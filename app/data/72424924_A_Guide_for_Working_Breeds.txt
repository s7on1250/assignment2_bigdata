"A Guide for Working Breeds" is a 2020 science fiction short story by Vina Jie-Min Prasad. It was first published in the anthology Made To Order: Robots and Revolution. ==Synopsis== Two robots — the newly-released K.g1-09030, and the far more experienced C.k2-00452, who is legally required to be K.g1's mentor — exchange a series of messages about dogs, computer vision, dogs, labor rights, dogs, cooking, dogs, the concept of cuteness, dogs, competitive murder, and dogs. ==Reception== "A Guide for Working Breeds" was a finalist for the 2021 Hugo Award for Best Short Story,2021 Hugo Awards, at TheHugoAwards.org; retrieved December 5, 2022 the Nebula Award for Best Short Story in 2020,A Guide for Working Breeds, at Science Fiction Writers of America; retrieved December 5, 2022 and the 2021 Theodore Sturgeon Award.Here Are the Finalists for the 2021 Theodore Sturgeon Memorial Award, by Andrew Liptak; at Tor.com; published July 7, 2021; retrieved December 5, 2022 Black Gate called it "hilarious" and "well worth [the reader's] time".New Treasures: Made To Order: Robots and Revolution edited by Jonathan Strahan, reviewed by John O'Neill, at Black Gate; published March 18, 2020; retrieved December 5, 2022 ==References== ==External links== *Text of the story, at Tor.com Category:Short stories about robots
