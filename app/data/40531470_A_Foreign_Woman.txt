A Foreign Woman () is a novel by the Russian writer Sergei Dovlatov. It is the first author's book about life in America. ==Plot introduction== The story is set in New York City in the 1980s and begins with a description of the narrator's neighbourhood and the Russian immigrants who live there. It then moves back in time and over to the Soviet Union to describe a young woman called Marusia Tatarovich who, in time, emigrates to United States. ==Publication history== The book was published in New York by Russica Publishers in 1986. ==References== Category:1986 Russian novels Category:Novels by Sergei Dovlatov Category:Novels set in New York City
